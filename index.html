Description

Victor, vfp@cin.ufpe.br
Tuesday, 17th April 2018

Public spending control and corruption of government members are problems encountered all over the world and this is an issue that machine learning has tried to help mitigate. Brazil is not exempt from these problems, taking 79th place in the ranking of the most corrupt countries in the world in 2017. However, the open data available on the chamber portal is a strong tool for identifying potential cases of corruption and undue expenditure.

Despite the efforts of multiple teams and public incentives for the development of systems capable of identifying corruption, the results found, such as Operação Serenata de Amor, are highly practical and have little academic character.

The objective of this research is to establish a model capable of extracting knowledge from a database of public spending, identifying patterns and finding in the mass of data potential cases of undue expenditure that point to situations of corruption.

The information available on the website of the Chamber of Deputies provides data on the expenses of the deputies of Brazil, composing a set of time series containing information on the use of money. In this context, it is planned to use time series forecasting and clustering techniques to draw a profile of CEAP expenditures [1] of Brazil's politicians. From this model it is expected that it is possible to identify the symptoms of corruption at non-standard points.

At the end of this research, it is expected to find a tool capable of identifying suspicious situations from any input expenditure, allowing it to be used for different types of spending and in different countries.
